The Anatomy of a Corrupt Project

Corruption in public works is not a single act but a systemic process. It involves collusion between public officials and private contractors at multiple stages, from budget allocation to project completion, to siphon public funds.

Key Actors & Their Roles

A complex network of individuals and groups facilitates systemic corruption. Understanding the role each one plays is crucial to identifying points of intervention and accountability.

Legislators

Influence budget allocations, inserting pet projects ("pork barrel") often in collusion with contractors. They may receive kickbacks for ensuring projects are funded and awarded to their chosen firms.

DPWH Officials

From regional directors to bidding committee members, they can manipulate the bidding process, approve substandard work, and falsify project completion reports in exchange for a share of the illicit funds.

Contractors

Form cartels to rig bids, ensuring projects are rotated among them. They pay kickbacks to officials and use low-quality materials to recover the "cost of doing business" and maximize their profits.

Local Government Officials

Act as facilitators on the ground. They can influence project selection, ignore substandard work within their jurisdiction, and help create "ghost projects" in collusion with contractors and DPWH personnel.
